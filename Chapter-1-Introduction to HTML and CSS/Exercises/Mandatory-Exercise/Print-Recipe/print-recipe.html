TIRAMISU Recipe

This Italian Tiramisu recipe consists of a sweet creamy mascarpone filling layered with espresso-dipped ladyfingers and topped with a thick dusting of cocoa powder. Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert, including the robust taste of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, and the subtle sweetness of whipped cream. The dessert is layered to perfection, offering a creamy texture and a delicate balance of sweetness from cream and bitterness from coffee. Whether you're celebrating a special occasion or indulging in a treat after a family meal, Tiramisu is sure to impress. With its elegant presentation and melt-in-your-mouth experience, it's perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Prep Time: 15 mins | Cooking Time: 30 mins | Total Time: 45 mins

Course: Dessert | Cuisine: Italian | Servings: 8

1. Ingredients

  • 6 large room temperature egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup of your personal favorite strong brewed coffee or espresso (cold)
  • 24 ladyfinger biscuits (Savoiardi)
  • 1 tablespoon of Dutch-processed cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • Dark, milk, or white chocolate shavings (optional)

2. Instructions

  • In a large bowl, whisk together 6 large egg yolks and 3/4 cup granulated sugar until the mixture is pale yellow and glossy for about 3 minutes.
  • In a shallow bowl, whisk together some cocoa powder, 1 cup of espresso or coffee (your choice), and 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract. Set aside so that the flavors infuse well together.
  • In a separate bowl, beat together 1 cup of mascarpone cheese, cream and gradually add the egg yolk mixture until almost combined.
  • Gently fold the mascarpone cheese into the egg yolk mixture until smooth.
  • After folding, make sure the batter holds a soft peak texture.
  • Dip each ladyfinger quickly into the espresso or coffee mixture (don't let them soak—just a quick dip!) and lay them in the bottom on both sides.
  • Make the first layer of ladyfinger biscuits by lining them in a baking dish and top the first layer with 1/2 of the mascarpone mixture.
  • Repeat the process by layering another set of dipped ladyfingers and spreading the remaining mascarpone mixture over the top.
  • Finish by dusting with cocoa powder on top and sprinkle with chocolate shavings (if desired).
  • Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving (overnight is best). Enjoy your homemade tiramisu!

3. Tips and Tricks

  • Make sure all the ingredients for the mascarpone mixture are cold.
  • For perfect Italian tiramisu, get the ladyfinger biscuits from an Italian store! And make sure to pick hard ones.
  • Mascarpone cheese is a must for the sour taste to go with sweet cream filling, so there is no substitution for it.
  • If you don't have espresso — no problem! You can substitute it with your favorite brewed coffee.
  • Dunking the ladyfinger biscuit is quite challenging. Make sure not to soak them for a long time as they will fall apart. A quick dunk will do the job perfectly—no more than 2-3 seconds!
  • Avoid serving it immediately! While it may taste great, the cocoa powder needs time to blend into the creamy topping. Otherwise, you'll end up breathing in dry cocoa—and trust me, that's not a pleasant experience! 😅
