Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert, including the robust taste of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, and the subtle sweetness of whipped cream. The dessert is layered to perfection, offering a creamy texture and a delicate balance of sweetness from cream and bitterness from coffee. Jump to Recipe

Ingredients you'll need:

  • 6 large room temperature egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup of strong brewed coffee or espresso (cold)
  • 24 ladyfinger biscuits (Savoiardi)
  • 1 tablespoon of Dutch-processed cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • Dark, milk, or white chocolate shavings (optional)

How to make Tiramisu:

  1. Whisk Eggs and Sugar

    In a large bowl, whisk together 6 large egg yolks and 3/4 cup granulated sugar until the mixture is pale yellow and glossy.

  2. Prepare Coffee Mixture

    In a shallow bowl, whisk together cocoa powder, 1 cup of espresso or coffee, and 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract.

  3. Prepare Mascarpone Mixture

    Beat together mascarpone cheese, heavy cream, and then slowly mix in the egg yolk mixture.

  4. Fold Ingredients

    Gently fold the mascarpone mixture until smooth and fluffy.

  5. Dip Ladyfingers

    Dip each ladyfinger briefly into the espresso mixture (avoid soaking to prevent sogginess).

  6. Assemble Layers

    Layer dipped ladyfingers and spread half of the mascarpone mixture over them. Repeat the process for a second layer.

  7. Dust with Cocoa

    Dust with cocoa powder and refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.

Tips and Tricks

  • Make sure all the ingredients for the mascarpone mixture must be cold.
  • For perfect Italian tiramisu, get the ladyfinger biscuits from an Italian store! And make sure to pick hard ones.
  • Mascarpone cheese is a must for the sour taste to go with sweet cream filling, so there is no substitution for it.
  • If you don't have espresso —no problem! You can substitute it with your favorite brewed coffee.
  • Dunking the ladyfinger biscuit is quite challenging. Make sure not to soak them for a long time as they will fall apart. A quick dunk will do the job perfectly—no more than 2-3 seconds!
  • Avoid serving it immediately! While it may taste great, the cocoa powder needs time to blend into the creamy topping. Otherwise, you'll end up breathing in dry cocoa—and trust me, that's not a pleasant experience! 😅

Tiramisu Recipe Summary

This Italian Tiramisu recipe consists of espresso-dipped ladyfingers layered with a sweet mascarpone filling and dusted with cocoa powder.

Prep Time: 15 mins | Total Time: 45 mins

Course: Dessert | Cuisine: Italian | Servings: 8
