How to Train Your Dragon

OVERVIEW

"Once you’ve earned his loyalty, there is nothing a dragon won’t do for you." – Hiccup

Set in the Viking village of Berk, How to Train Your Dragon follows Hiccup, an outcast who discovers that dragons are not the dangerous creatures his tribe believes them to be. Through his bond with Toothless, he changes the way Vikings and dragons coexist.

The film explores themes of friendship, acceptance, and bravery. As Hiccup trains Toothless, he learns that understanding and compassion are more powerful than fear and violence. How to Train Your Dragon is a heartfelt journey of discovery, not just for Hiccup, but for the entire village of Berk.

Key Information

Title: How to Train Your Dragon
Release Year: 2010
Genre: Animation, Fantasy, Adventure
Runtime: 98 minutes
Studio: DreamWorks Animation
Director: Chris Sanders, Dean DeBlois
Producers: Bonnie Arnold
Screenplay: Chris Sanders, Dean DeBlois
Music By: John Powell

Box Office Performance

The movie grossed $494.9 million worldwide, making it one of the most successful animated films of 2010.

Awards & Nominations

How to Train Your Dragon was nominated for two Academy Awards, including Best Animated Feature and Best Original Score. It also won 10 Annie Awards, the most for any animated film that year.

Fun Facts

Toothless' flying animations were inspired by cats and dogs.
The dragon language was based on elephant sounds mixed with other animal noises.
John Powell’s soundtrack was nominated for Best Original Score at the Oscars.
